What is essay writing?

It is written composition to giving expression to one's own personal idea or opnions on some topic; but the term "essay" usually covers also any written composition, whether it expresses personal opinion , gives the information on any subject, or details of a narrative or descriptive.

Charactersitics of essay Writing?

A good essay must contain the following charactersitics:

  • Unity(means each paragraph has only one main idea)
  • Order(the essay should be in orderly manner of a subject)
  • Brevity ( brevity means write less and say more) or It should be concisely written not too long.
  • Style (style should be simple , direct and clear)
  • Personal Touch (it should be an indiviualty , or personal touch of writer).

Classification of Essay Writing

Essay map be classified as:

Narrative

In the narrative essay the writer tells the story about a real-life experiance. the narrative essay chellenges students to think and write themselves. when writting a narrative essay, the writers should try to involve the reader by making the story as vivid as possible.

Descriptive

A descriptive essay paints a picture with words. A writer might describe a person,place,object or even memomry of special significance.The descriptive essay strives to communicate a deeper meaning through the description.

Expository

In an expository essay, the writer explains or defines a topic, using facts,statistics and examples.expository writing encompasses(contain) the wide range of essay variations,such as the comparison and contrast essay, the cause and effect essay, and the "how to" or process essay.

Reflective

A reflective essay consists of reflections or thoughts on some topic, which is gernally of an abstract nature; for example; (a) habits , qualities (b) social, political and domestic topics (c) Philosophical subjects (d) religious and theological topics.

Imaginative

Essays on subjects such as feelings and experiances of the sailor wrecked on a desert island may be called imaginative essays. In such the writer is called to place himself in imagination in a position of which he has had no actual experiance. such subjects as "If I was a king," or "The autobiography of a horse," would call for imaginative essays.
